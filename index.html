1. Purpose of this Platform

2. Trade License Requirement (UAE Law)

MANDATORY: Under UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 32/2021 on Commercial Companies and the UAE Commercial Transactions Law, any person or entity offering commercial services within the UAE must hold a valid trade license issued by the relevant authority:
  • Mainland businesses: Department of Economic Development (DED) of the respective emirate.
  • Free zone businesses: The relevant free zone authority (e.g., DMCC, DAFZA, ADGM, DIFC, etc.).
  • Freelancers: A freelance permit issued by an authorised body (e.g., Dubai Creative Clusters, Sharjah Media City, twofour54, etc.).
  • Home-based businesses: A home-based business license from the DED or equivalent authority.

Operating without a license may result in fines of up to AED 100,000, business closure, legal prosecution, and potential visa implications for expatriates. The Platform does not verify the license status of any listed provider. Users are solely responsible for ensuring their own compliance.

If you are between jobs or starting out: Many emirates offer affordable options. Dubai's Instant License (AED 1,070/year) and Abu Dhabi's micro-business licenses are fast, low-cost paths to legal compliance. Free zone freelance permits can start from AED 5,750/year. We strongly encourage all community members to operate legally.
